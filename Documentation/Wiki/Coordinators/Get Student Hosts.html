Important Steps
 Key Points Reasons for Key Points
Invite students to meeting for student hosts.
  • Advertise with clubs, BA3250, and any other classes that are related to the seminar topic.
  • The Business Ambassadors are now ready and expecting to be a part of the conference student hosting. Contact them and they will try to fill any remaining student hosting spots. 
  • Find as many people as possible to student host your seminar
  • Meetings should be held at night because more students are able to attend.
  • Have Assistant Director send out email to BUS 3250 class using the Student Host Email (or word doc) example with the information updated
  • The more people you can get to the meeting, the bigger pool of people you can pick from. 

Have meeting with potential student hosts.
  • If possible look over resumes to determine what students would be best, or ensure the volunteering students will be professional (very important!).
  • Be professional as you explain requirements and expectations. 
  • Student hosts are a good pool from which to choose future coordinators.
  • Student hosts are a direct reflection of the professionalism of Partners in Business
Save Student Host information into Logistics database
  • Use the GoogleDoc, Student Hosting Spots, to pre-assign any students you want for specific speakers
  • Also use the GoogleDoc to have students sign up for remaining peakers. Copy link and send the link to students (don't need gmail account to access)
  • Logistical database:  Speakers 2010.accdb
  • This will make coordinating more convenient for you so you don't have to call back and forth all the time (especially with business ambassadors) 
  • This will tie together student hosts with their respective speakers (and create easy to access information for the future).
Hold a meeting with selected student hosts.
  • Students will receive a Student Host Packet at this meeting.  The packet includes a copy of the Student Host Responsibilities, an example of a good introduction, the logistics and detailed travel itinerary, speaker biography and presentation summary, and an airport sign, if applicable.
  • Explain and demonstrate a good introduction for a speaker Good Example: OGIO Intro.docx
  • Dress and act professionally and answer any questions for the student hosts
  • This meeting can be done one-on-one for those who can't attend the group meeting.
  • The Student Host Responsibilities sheet lists the responsibilities of a student host, as well as the information you need to provide for them.
  • Emphasize the importance of acting professional.  This includes practicing a well-written introduction.  Attendees often comment on the professionalism (or lack of) of the introduction.  Practice, practice, practice!  And take time to craft an original, personal, interesting intro.  Anyone can read the biography sketch in the daybook.
Create and hand out airport sign for speakers who are being picked up from the airport.
  • Download the Airport Speaker Names Template and change the names for each of your speakers that are being picked up from the airport.
  • This helps the speakers to easily find the student host.
