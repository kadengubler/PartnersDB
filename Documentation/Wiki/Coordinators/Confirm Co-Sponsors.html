Find Co-Sponsors
  • Look at past co-sponsors (they can be found in the previous year's daybook front cover).
  • Invite co-sponsors from the previous year to continue as a co-sponsor for this year's conference.
  • Research non-profit organizations related to your conference.
  • We benefit from non-profit co-sponsors by getting their mailing lists to market our seminars.  The more their members' interests are aligned with the seminar, the better.
  • Non-profit organizations benefit by increased exposure for their organization amongst our seminar attendees.

Invite Co-sponsor

 Tell the benefits of being a co-sponsor:
  • One free attendance to the conference.
  • Discounted rate of $50 per day ($100 per day for the OE conference)  
  • Listing in the daybook given to each attendee
  • Booth at the conference
  • Promotional slide for the organizations to be shown between speakers.
  • Link to their website on Partners in Business site
  • Free lunch and parking
  • You can also send them this document via email Co-sponsor Benefits.doc

Describe what is expected by being a co-sponsor:

  • Use of e-mail list to be used several times to promote the conference.  If they cannot give us their e-mail list, ask if they will forward the email onto their contact list if we send it to them.  This may be a better option because the email will come with a recommendation from a trusted source (the organization).
  • If the relationship goes over smooth, consider asking them for a letter of support for the Partners In Business Program.
