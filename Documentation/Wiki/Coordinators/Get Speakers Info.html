Email with Speaker Deadlines
  • Send the Speaker Deadline Form to your speakers one week after confirming them as a speaker.
  • The document lists deadlines in terms of days or weeks before the seminar--update these deadlines to the specific due dates.
  • List the important upcoming dates into the body of the email.  Include a "heads up" of what is in the attachment, so the speaker will open, read, and understand why it's important.
  • Make sure the speaker knows that this document will be coming in an attachment. 
  • This form works well.

 

 

 

 

 

 

  • Many people are hesitant to open attachments that they are not expecting.  Speakers need to know what is coming and why it's important.

Input data into Itinerary Form
  • Enter the speaker information on the Itinerary Form, Speakers 2010.accdb.
  • This will reduce the amount of work when it comes time to make the seminar forms.
Save all forms you receive in the individual speaker's folder.
  • Create folders for each speaker, named by the speaker's name.
  • Save all forms which relate to the specific speaker in his/her folder.
  • This is a way to standardize document collection.  It is very helpful when coordinators use  the same filling system.
Be persistent and continually follow up until you have all the necessary info.
  • Change methods of contact:  phone, email, and less frequently, letters, are all good ways of contacting.
  • If you send a letter, it should be personalized, and you should call to make sure the speaker received it.
  • It is essential to follow up periodically with your speakers.  Speakers will forget about the deadlines, so remind them as the deadlines approach.  Using different methods of contacting the speakers will gets more commitment.
