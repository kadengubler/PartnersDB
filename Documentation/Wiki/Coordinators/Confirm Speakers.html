Important Steps
 Key Points Reasons for Key Points
Find Topics
  • Base this on past evaluations
  • Look for similar successful events and possibly use similar topics
  • Talk to professors and professionals to get a feel for what would be popular.
  • Use your professional organizations for help in deciding who to invite. For the OE conference use the Shingo Prize and for accounting use the school of accountancy. 
  • The better the topics are, the more attendance you will have
Find Speakers
  • Look for speakers that are well known in the topics desired for the conference
  • Places to look:
    • Look for speakers that have spoken in the past and had good reviews. (Try not to repeat speakers that presented within the last two conferences)
    • Ask people for recommendations
    • Similar Conferences
    • Google
    • Amazon (for Authors)
    • Magazines (don't get lost reading non-relevant articles) a better option is to use Business Source Premier to find magazine articles that are relevant
  • Entertaining and well respected speakers will help Partners in Business in the future
Invite Speakers

Example Invite OE.docx 
Example Invitation TEMPLATE.doc

 
  • Know the reasons Partners wants them to speak (recognize accomplishments, achievements, experience).
  • Know the reasons speakers will want to speak (benefits of speaking).

Get Confirmation
  • If the speaker doesn't send confirmation after 1 week, contact them again.  Preferably by phone
  • Continue to contact until you have a confirmation or declination. Be pleasantly persistent, but not annoying.
  • If the speaker declines send a thank you email for considering and ask if they would you like to speak in the future.
  • Let the speakers know ahead of time to keep their presentation slides SIMPLE, so when they are printed 3 to a page in the daybook, they are still readable.  (Preferably larger font)
  • Keep slides to a minimum because too much information takes away from their presentation.
  • It is essential that you get a strong confirmation from the speakers. 

 
Payment Info
  • All Speaker Fees must be approved by the assistant director
    • This includes travel expenses
  • Do not offer any monetary compensation without talking to the assistant director.
    • If the topic comes up ask them what their normal fees are
    • Ask if they work with non-profit organizations on the rates
    • Ask if they would be willing to donate their fee to partners after we have paid them
    • Tell them that you need approval to give any compensation
  • The most that Partners has ever paid for a speaker is $10,000 Plus travel
    • The speakers that have received $10,000 have brought in many more attendees, and have been very prestigious speakers
  
Track Speakers that you invited and confirmed on a spreadsheet
  • Use the Speaker Tracking Sheet Template.xlsx You can modify or color code as you wish.
  • At the end of your conference, you will include some of this information on the Potential Speaker Template so keep track of it so you can fill out this form.
  • This will help future coordinators and eliminate time spent researching speakers that may have already been invited.
  • This will help the next coordinator pick up on speakers that were interested, but couldn't fit it into their schedule.
