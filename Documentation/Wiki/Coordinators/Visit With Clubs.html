 Key Points Reasons for Key Points

Visit with clubs
  • There are tons of clubs within the Huntsman School of Business.  Research and choose a couple clubs with interests  related to the theme of your seminar to visit.
  • Partners prides itself on its inclusion of USU business students in the seminar program.  Visiting with clubs is a wonderful way to get good students interested in attending and student hosting for your seminar.
Talk to Club Advisors about Dinner
  • Reach out to the club advisor or president (often a student) to get an interest in members attending and networking at the PIB dinner for speakers. 
  • The club president or advisors are responsible to invite their members and collect money from the students who will attend. The clubs should provide name badges for their students.
  • Students, faculty, and speakers will be split up around each table.
  • This is a great opportunity for the students to build their network, make personal connections with professionals, and gain insights from very interesting, accomplished people!
Provide general information about the Partners in Business Program to student clubs and ask for help when appropriate.
  • Inform members about  upcoming seminars
  • Explain the purpose of Partners in Business to club members
  • Describe the seminar(s) that relate best to the specific club
  • Inform members about the opportunities as a student host and seminar coordinator and encourage participation/involvement!
  • Clubs are a good way to reach the students interested in extracurricular activities.  Partners is always looking to increase student interest, attendance, and fill hosting and coordinating opportunities.
Create ongoing relationships
  • Become a member of the club, if possible
  • Attend/visit the club frequently
  • Help the club get guest speakers--use connections with PIB speakers coming into town when there is a good opportunity or fit.
  • Creating a solid relationship with students is vital to successfully increasing effective student involvement.
