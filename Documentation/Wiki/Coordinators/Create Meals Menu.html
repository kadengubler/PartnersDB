Look over options! (Catering Menu)
  • Ask the Assistant Director for a copy of the menu.
  • You can find a current version of the menu on the catering website: http://catering.usu.edu/htm/menu
  • There will be a record of previous meals.  Partners staff can offer great insight into what was successful and what was  not as yummy. :)
Choose meals for your seminar.
  • Meals should be less than $14 per plate
  • If you have a multiple-day seminar, do not repeat the same meal.
  • Be sure to order Coffee for breakfast and lunch
 
Give menu to Assistant Director    

Speakers are invited to a special dinner for seminar speakers and PIB staff. 

 

Talk to the Assistant Director about dinner with speakers and choose a venue.

 

The dinner will be the night before a one-day seminar, or the night in between a multiple-day seminar.
  • Local restaurants work best.
  • Avoid chain restaurants like Chili's or Olive Garden.
  • Choose a restaurant that facilitates a good environment for conversation.  For example, Cafe Sabor is great if you can reserve a private room or sit outside, otherwise it is too loud/noisy and is not be the best choice.
  • Avoid chain restaurants like Chili's or Olive Garden.  Show speakers a little bit of Cache Valley flavor they won't experience elsewhere!  Some examples include Cafe Sabor, Hamiltons, Blackstone, Coppermill, etc..
Assistant Director will call for reservations.
  • The Assistant Director has this task in his/her standardized work (Complete One Week Before Stuff), but once you have chosen a location with him/her, remind the Assistant Director to make the reservations.
